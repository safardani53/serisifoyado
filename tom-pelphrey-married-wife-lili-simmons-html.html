Tall, good looks and great acting abilities have made Tom Pelphrey one of the top rated actors of today. His amazing acting ability has been well received by the audiences and Tom has been showered with heaps of praises and admirations. 

However, when it comes to his love life, it has been anything but a smooth ride for Tom. Pelphrey after suffering the ignominy of a failed relationship gathered himself up for love. And soon after the breakup, he had a new love interest by his side when he attended an event in New York.

The native of Howell Township, New Jersey is best known as Jonathan Randall on the soap opera, Guiding Light, which aired on CBS. The soap opera is listed in Guinness World Records as the longest-running drama in television in American history.

After his stint at Guiding Light, he has been appearing as Ward Meachum on Netflix's web television series, Iron Fist.

For his performance on Guiding Light series, Tom won two Daytime Emmy Award for Outstanding Younger Actor in a Drama Series in 2006 and 2008. He has also earned nominations for various awards such as Gold Derby Award and Soap Opera Digest Award thanks to Guiding Light. 

Tom Pelphrey's Bio: Family, Height, Education

The versatile actor was born as Thomas J. Pelphrey to an American family on 28 July 1972 in Howell, New Jersey. His parents are Laurie Pelphrey, Richard Pelphrey and he also has a brother named Robert Pelphrey in the family.

At the age of 18, he completed his high school education at Howell High School in 2000. Later, he got graduated with a Bachelor of Fine Arts degree from Rutgers University in 2004.

Started Acting Career At Age 25

Unlike many famed actors who start their acting career at an early age, Tom was a latecomer to the acting scene. He began his acting career at the age of 25 portraying the character of Douche Bag Dave on a series, The Burg in 2007. Afterward, he was cast on a 2008 comedy-drama movie, Birds of America.

His role as David on a thriller movie, Junction yield an Award for Best Ensemble Cast at ReelHeart International Film Festival in 2012. Some of his notable on-screen work includes, As the World Turns (2009), Banshee (2015) and Chicago P.D (2017).

Apart from acting, Tom also lent his voice for the character as Connor J. Morgan on a 2011 video game, Homefront. Tom has also demonstrated his acting skill in various theatres.

Tom performed in Kevin Mandel's A New Television Arrives Finally portraying the title character as television at Theatre 54 in New York in September 2007. He was cast in In God's Hat, portraying the character of Roy performed at the Peter Jay Sharp Theater in New York City in August 2010.

In 2012, he appeared in the Broadway cast of the musical drama End of the Rainbow landing the role as Mickey Deans. The drama focuses on the late American actress Judy Garland in the months leading up to her death in 1969.

After three years, he returned to Broadway playing Martin in a revival of Fool for Love, written by American playwright and actor Sam Shepard in 2015.
