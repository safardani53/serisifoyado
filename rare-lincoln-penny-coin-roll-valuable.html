I found one of the rarest Lincoln pennies in a nickel coin roll - the exact year that could make it

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Thursday, March 7, 2024

A COIN collector on YouTube unexpectedly found an antique penny while sorting through rolls of nickels in a recent video.

Ryan Quinlan, who runs the YouTube channel Quin's Coins, was shocked to find the rare penny amid five-cent pieces.

The coin - a 1918 wheat cent - features the face of 16th US President Abraham Lincoln.

Lincoln was added to the penny in 1909 to commemorate his 100th birthday.

He has been a mainstay on the cent ever since, but this 1918 coin is among the most valuable in the Lincoln series.

What is a wheat cent?

Every penny produced from 1909 until 1958 was a wheat cent.

The coins got their name from the design on the reverse, or tails side.

Victor David Brenner, the coin's designer, depicted two stalks of wheat on the backside of the coin.

Each wheat cent also features the words "E Pluribus Unum," as well as its value and country of origin on the reverse side.

The obverse, or heads side, of every wheat cent is President Lincoln's face, surrounded by the words "Liberty" and "In God We Trust."

Wheat pennies also include the year they were minted to the right of Lincoln's chin.

Brenner's wheat reverse side was replaced in 1959 with an image of the Lincoln Memorial that remains standard on modern pennies.

Value of 1918 wheat cents

Wheat cents from 1918 hold a unique place in the history of one-cent coins.

On Brenner's original 1909 Lincoln wheat cent design, he engraved his initials on the bottom of the reverse side of the coin.

After the design generated some outrage from journalists, Brenner's initials were removed and millions of 1909 pennies were made without them.

In 1918, however, Brenner's initials returned to the coin in a less conspicuous place - the VDB can be seen faintly on Lincoln's shoulder.

According to the Professional Coin Grading Service (PCGS), 1918 pennies can be worth anywhere from a few dollars to a few thousand.

How much an individual coin is worth depends on its condition and where it was minted.

The US issued one-cent pieces from the Philadelphia, Denver, and San Francisco Mints in 1918.

San Francisco coins hold the most value, according to PCGS auction data.

They're worth up to $100 in circulated condition, while mint state coins are worth upwards of $2,000.

Ryan's coin was minted in Philadelphia and can be worth hundreds of dollars, according to PCGS data.

More tips for finding valuable change

There are many ways you can find rare coins.

You’ll first want to check around your home or any places you might store change.

You can also try using a metal detector outdoors, or stopping by your local bank and asking for a roll of coins.

Banks allow you to exchange bills for rolls of coins at no cost, giving you plenty of change to sort through for standouts.

To check if your coins are worth anything on eBay, search the full name, select the "sold" listing and then toggle the search to "highest value”.

This way, you'll know what collectors are willing to pay for your coins.

You can either choose to sell the coin on eBay or through a specialist site such as Coinappraiser or USA Coin Book.

And be aware of potential fakes when buying online.

To avoid this, it's worth checking the seller’s history and whether or not the coin was certified in the listing.
