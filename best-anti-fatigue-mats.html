Things to Consider Before Buying an Anti-Fatigue Mat

Material

Anti-fatigue mats are typically made from foam, but getting down to the nitty-gritty of your mat makeup can help you select the best one. "Anti-fatigue or standing mats are designed to offload stress to the foot and body associated with prolonged standing. Some mats provide cushion, whereas others provide irregular surfaces or texture to stimulate and massage the feet," says New York City podiatrist Emily Splichal, D.P.M.

Uber-comfortable anti-fatigue mats are often made from memory foam, which is ideal for shoppers looking for a surface to comfortably contour to the shape of their feet for support and comfort — not unlike how memory foam mattresses cushion the body. Another popular material is biofoam, which claims to release energy back into your ligaments to activate the muscles in your footbed as you stand (take the GelPro NewLife EcoPro, for example). Additionally, your mat's exterior can complicate your cleaning process, as textured surfaces hold more grime than smoother ones.

Thickness

Most anti-fatigue mats range from 0.25 to 1 inch thick. The right thickness is a matter of personal preference. The most comfortable and supportive anti-fatigue mats are generally at least 0.5 inches thick, and commercial grade mats typically don't drop below 0.38 inches, so anything thinner likely won't provide you with longterm support. You’ll also want to consider the amount of time you’ll be standing on the mat each day and whether or not you’ll be wearing shoes (which also add support and cushioning) when deciding on thickness. 

Size

To choose the right size mat, measure the size of the area where you’ll be using it. If you have a large, open area, you may opt for a larger mat that allows you to move around freely without stepping on and off the mat. Alternatively, if you plan to use your anti-fatigue mat in front of the kitchen sink or under a standing desk, you can choose a smaller mat that just fits that footprint.

Purpose

Just as different rooms have different purposes, the anti-fatigue mat you choose for different areas should have unique roles as well. For example, you wouldn't want an oddly-shaped standing mat to be the style you choose for a bustling kitchen, just like you wouldn't want a mat that's too large under your desk. However, it is important to keep an eye out for non-slip mats no matter which room it ends up in, as they should all serve the purpose of keeping you upright.

Style

After all, an anti-fatigue mat is part of your room, so it should match your personal style or the atmosphere of your space, which is why it may be important to choose a mat that has multiple color and pattern options (we suggest the House of Noa Nama Standing Mat for an aesthetically-pleasing print or the Gorilla Grip mat for color options). This way, you can find a style that suits you and looks like it belongs in your room.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • How do anti-fatigue mats work?

    Anti-fatigue mats work by providing a cushion between your feet and the floor, much like how the best insoles provide an extra layer of comfort between your feet and soles of your shoes. If you stand for long periods of time, whether cooking in the kitchen or working at a standing desk, an anti-fatigue mat can reduce the discomfort caused by standing for long periods of time on a hard surface, similar to the function of men's and women's work shoes.

  • What is a good thickness for an anti-fatigue mat?

    Anti-fatigue mats are available in a range of thicknesses from 0.25 to 1 inch. The thickest mat that we tested was 0.90 inches, almost hitting that density ceiling (and if you didn't pick up on it before, it's also our cheapest option). As a reference, industrial-grade mats should be at least 0.38 inches thick, so you probably don't want anything thinner.

  • How do you clean anti-fatigue mats?

    While you should check for any special care instructions from the manufacturer, most of these mats can be cleaned simply using soap, water, and a cloth or sponge. It is advisable to let your mat dry completely before putting it back on the floor to prevent trapping moisture between it and the floor. Make sure you keep an eye out for mats that have low-maintenance surfaces, like the Amazon Basics mat that has a rubber surface, which allows spills to be hosed or wiped right off.

  • What are the health benefits of anti-fatigue mats?

    These tools alleviate the impact transferred to the joints and spinal column when walking and standing on hard floors. They also promote better circulation by cushioning the feet, which helps reduce issues later in the lower limbs. It's really no different than putting on a pair of supportive cushioned running shoes before hitting the pavement for a long-distance jog. Splichal recommends "looking for a mat that can activate, release, and offload the foot during prolonged standing." She goes on to explain that the ball of the foot and heel can often feel the most stress from prolonged standing, as this is where the most pressure is placed, so finding a mat that provides even cushioning is key.

  • Can you use an anti-fatigue mat on a hardwood floor?

    Yes. In fact, they are ideal mats to use on hardwood flooring because they are scratch-resistant, water-resistant, and easy to maintain. Just make sure you're picking a tapered edge, non-slip mat to avoid any falls or accidents.

How We Tested

After scouring the internet, researching, and reading countless reviews, we selected 16 of the most popular anti-fatigue mats to test in our PEOPLE Tested lab. Once we had the mats in front of us, we picked apart each mat by the numbers to get all the specifications recorded — from inside and outside materials to dimensions and thickness (and everything in between).

We then evaluated the comfort of these mats by placing them at the feet of testers in the kitchen and having them stand on each for an hour as they prepped and cooked meals. After this, the testers were asked to assess how their feet, ankles, knees, hips, back, and shoulders felt.

Once the initial standing tests were done, next came the cleaning test. Each mat was dirtied with a dusting of all-purpose flour, drizzled with black strap molasses, and left to sit out and harden for 12 hours — so we got the real low-down on how these mats clean off. Next came the cleaning attempt, where they were rinsed or scrubbed off (depending on how much the mat wanted to hold onto the mess).

After each test, we evaluated each anti-fatigue mat based on its performance in terms of design, comfort, and ease of cleaning, rating each on a scale of 1 to 5 based on performance (1 being the poorest and 5 being the best). From there, we took the average scores across each category to give each mat its editorial rating, which gave us our clear winners — the joint-saving mats you've read about above.
