Logan Paul scammed into spending $3.5million on FAKE Pokemon Cards as boxer was duped into buying ol

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Wednesday, June 19, 2024

LOGAN Paul was scammed into spending $3.5million on fake Pokemon Cards as he was duped into buying old G.I. Joe cards.

The YouTube sensation and boxing icon — who has amassed a collection of cards over the years — took to Twitter on Thursday afternoon to share a new video revealing how he was tricked.

"New video, this sucks. I Lost $3,500,000 On Fake Pokémon Cards watch so I can get my money back," he tweeted.

The Youtuber turned boxer posted the video on his channel revealing the huge purchase to his audience.

The video, titled I Lost $3,500,000 On Fake Pokémon Cards, has already obtained more than 1.5million views.

The seven-minute video, which was filmed in Chicago in December 2021, shows Paul meeting with authentication company BBCE.

Once the box was opened, one of the authenticators said: "Why are they so puffy? See how they're shaped all — they don't look right.

"See how they're curvy and puffy. They're very light. They don't look right."

At that moment Paul is seen holding his head and saying: "Oh My God."

Once the cards are grabbed, Paul looks on in fear and says: "Oh, no... no, no, no. Why is it bending like that? Why is it bending like that? What's inside of them?"

He quickly adds: "It's fake, bro. It's fake."

When the box is opened, they realize the cards inside are G.I. Joe cards.

"G.I. Joe! It could have been anything else," Paul yelled.

After one of the verifiers admits they "got duped, another called the incident "the biggest fraud in the entire history of Pokemon."

The video concludes with Paul explaining: I'm a super positive person, bro.

"And, I'll always be the one to look at the bright side, and I am trying, but this is very hard.

"It's just so sad for all parties involved. It's sad for the Pokemon community.

"Like, how many fraudulent things are out there? I'm grateful for the things I have that are real."

Paul said he went from having 11 first edition Pokemon boxes to five as a result of this scam.

Despite his loss, he still has a first edition "Shadowless" Charizard card, TMZ reports.

Paul claims the card is now worth more than a million dollars.

He also now only has five packs of first edition Pokémon cards.

Thankfully, Logan's friend/famous card collector Bolillo Lajan San revealed to TMZ that Paul did indeed get his money back.

"I have reimbursed Logan his 3.5," Bolillo Lajan San said to TMZ.

Meanwhile, many users took to the comments section of the YouTube video to express their thoughts on the situation.

One Youtuber wrote: "I remember buying a fake $10,000 box and being upset…but $3,500,000 I can’t imagine."
