Billie Eilishs No Time To Die James Bond theme song explained from lyrics to Easter eggs

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Sunday, March 10, 2024

BILLIE Eilish sent James Bond fans wild with the release of the theme song to No Time To Die.

The 18-year-old Grammy winner performed the title song for the 25th Bond movie at the 2020 Brit Awards before giving an emotional acceptance speech after winning the International Solo Female Artist gong.

When was Billie Eilish's No Time To Die released?

It was first revealed back in January 2019 that Billie would be recording the song for the next James Bond film, No Time To Die.

The song was then released on February 14, 2020 - which you can watch at the top of this article.

Through her record label Billie said: “It feels crazy to be a part of this in every way. To be able to score the theme song to a film that is part of such a legendary series is a huge honour.

"James Bond is the coolest film franchise ever to exist. I’m still in shock."

What's the meaning behind Billie Eilish's No Time To Die?

The song has lots of hidden Bond Easter eggs in it.

With this being Daniel Craig’s final turn as James Bond, there are a few lines in the No Time To Die that seem to signal the end of him as 007.

As many have speculated from the trailer, it seems that Léa Seydoux’s Madeleine Swann could end up betraying Bond.

The lines “Was I stupid to love you? Was I reckless to help? Was it obvious to everybody else?” and “The blood you bleed is just your own,” all point to a deadly end for our Bond.

When is the James Bond film No Time To Die out in cinemas?

The film will be released in UK cinemas on April 2, 2020.

The action film is directed by Cary Joji Fukunaga, who has helmed box office hits such as Jane Eyre while also holding writing credits to movie titles including 2017's It.

It's not yet known the running time or age rating.
