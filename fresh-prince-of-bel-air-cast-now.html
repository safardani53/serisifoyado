What happened to Fresh Prince of Bel-Air stars - from Will Smiths feud with co-star to tragic dea

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Saturday, March 16, 2024

THE Fresh Prince is back - 25 years after Will Smith waved goodbye to the role that made his name.

The trailer for Bel-Air, the long-awaited reboot of the the 90s sitcom, dropped this week and the show will air next month.

The Fresh Prince of Bel-Air premiered in September 1990 and aired its final series in 1996.

It followed the life of a young boy named Will, who moved from West Philadelphia to Los Angeles to live with his rich aunty and uncle in their mansion, after getting into trouble at home.

Will Smith - who is executive producer of the new show - went on to Hollywood superstardom with roles in box office behemoths like Men in Black, I Am Legend and the Oscar-nominated Ali.

But what have the rest of the cast been up to since?

Most of them showed up for a reunion in 2020, but here's what the other stars of the show did next...

Alfonso Ribeiro

Alfonso, 50, portrayed the much-loved Carlton Banks on the show.

Although he annoyed his cousin Will at times, fans loved to watch their relationship unfold on screen.

During his six years on the sitcom, his alter-ego coined "the Carlton", a dance which is still known to this day.

Since then he's appeared in various films and programmes as characters and himself.

Some include animated TV show Extreme Ghostbusters, Disney's Shake It Up and Nickelodeon's Big Time Rush.

He's also dipped his feet into the realm of reality TV, starring in The Graham Norton Show, ITV's I'm A Celebrity, BBC'S Strictly and Dancing with the Stars.

He's also directed a number of things including sitcom All of Us, Meet the Browns and K.C Undercover.

Alfonso was married to his first wife Robin Staple from 2002 to 2006, and they share a daughter together.

He went on to tie the knot with Angela Unkrich in 2012 and has three children with her - three children: Alfonso Lincoln Ribeiro Jr, eight, Anders Reyn Ribeiro, six, and Ava Sue Ribeiro, two.

Tatyana Ali

Tatyana, 42, shot to fame in 1985 as one of the kids in children's TV show Sesame Street.

After a number of roles she began to portray Ashley Banks, Will's little cousin, from 1990 to 1996.

It's not a shock that since then she's starred in other TV shows including Fastlane, Love That Girl!, Zoe Ever After and 413 Hope St.

Not only that, she's appeared in blockbusters such as Jawbreaker, Back In The Day and most recently Christmas Hotel.

The star is also extremely clever and graduated from Harvard University with a BA in African-American Studies and Government in 2002.

She tied the knot with Dr. Vaughn Rasberry in 2016 and they share two sons Edwards and Alejandro.

Joseph Marcell

Joseph, 73, has juggled his career between the UK and US since shooting to fame in 1974.

He's most known for playing the Banks' family butler Geoffrey Butler.

But after The Fresh Prince of Bel-Air ended he wasn't short of roles as he starred in American sitcoms Living Single, In The House and Bold and Beautiful.

He also returned back to the UK to play Vernon Johnson in The Bill, Aubrey Valentine in EastEnders, Carl Webster in Holby City and Alexander Jackson in Death in Paradise.

He most recently starred in Netflix's Ratched and The Man In The Hat.

Joseph also loves performing on stage and has been in Gem of the Ocean in 2016, Lady Windermere's Fan in 2018 and The Tempest in 2016.

James Avery

James is best known for playing Will's serious uncle Philip Banks in The Fresh Prince of Bel-Air.

The star sadly died in 2013 aged 69 after complications from open heart surgery.

Will Smith paid tribute to the actor, saying: "Some of my greatest lessons in acting, living, and being a respectable human being came through James Avery. Every young man needs an Uncle Phil. Rest in peace."

The actor had an extremely successful career throughout his life after shooting to fame in 1980 in The Stunt Man.

In 1997 he played Dr. Glidden in film Spirit Lost, a number of character's voices in The Prince of Egypt in 1998 and Eldon in Dr. Doo Little 2 are to name a few film roles.

TV wise James starred in a long list of shows including That's So Raven, Grey's Anatomy, CSI: Crime Scene Investigation and Star Trek: Enterprise.

He also voiced Br'er Bear in the video game Kinect Disneyland Adventures, Catalysm and Cat Comando in Biker Mice from Mars.

Janet Hubert

Janet, 65, was the first woman to take on the role of Will's aunt Vivian Banks.

She played the retired doctor for a total of epsiodes between 1990 and 1993.

It was reported that she left the show after clashing with co-star Will.

She was pregnant through the third season of the show, resulting in the arrival of Nicky, the Banks' youngest child being added to the script.

She later claimed she worked until two days before the birth only to be replaced in series four by Daphne Maxwell-Reid.

Will Smith claimed she had wanted a bigger role in the show and added: "She’s mad now but she’s been mad all along. She said once, 'I’ve been in the business for 10 years and this snotty-nosed punk comes along and gets a show.’ No matter what, to her I’m just the Antichrist.”

But Janet sued NBC and Smith for defamation,invasion of privacy, negligence and intentional infliction of emotional distress.

Her suit alleged that Smith became hostile after she got pregnant and lobbied NBC to reduce her salary and screen-time, and frequently flipped the finger at her.

The pair made up after airing their differences in an episode of Red Table Talk.

After Fresh Prince she bagged herself a number of TV roles after including in Friends, Gilmore Girls and One Life to Live.

The star also starred in films such as White Man's Burden, Proud and most recently Christmas Belles

She shares Elijah Isaac Whitten with her ex-husband James and married her second husband Larry Kraft in 2005.

Ross Bagley

Ross was already known for the movie The Little Rascals and was actually five when he made his season three debut as Nicky - a role he played until the show ended in 1996.

He went on to become a child star, appearing in Independence Day, with Will Smith and Eye For An Eye, as well as numerous small TV roles.

He also voiced a puppy in the 1995 classic Babe.

Ross now works as a real estate agent in Los Angeles and DJs on the side.

Daphne Maxwell-Reid

Daphne, 73, played Vivian Banks from 1993 to 1996.

She shot to fame in 1979 as Dr Karla Meredith in A Man Called Sloane.

The actress played roles in a long list of TV shows before The Fresh Prince of Bel-Air including Murder, She Wrote, Frank's Place and The Long Journey Home.

Away from the screen, Daphne is an accomplished photographer and dress designer.

She has also owned her own studio, New Millennium Studios in Virginia, which she ran with second husband Tim Reid until 2014.

Karyn Parson

Karyn, 54, took on the role of Will's oldest cousin Hilary Banks from 1990 to 1996.

She went on to play Margot Hines in Lush Life, Jackie Zambrano in Melrose Place and Elaine in Linc's.

From 2002 to 2017 Karyn, who is married to director Alexandre Rockwell, stayed out of the spotlight and in that time gave birth to her two children Lana, 17, and Nico, 14.

She's also published two children books How High the Moon and Flying Free.

Jeffrey Allen Townes

Jeffrey, 56 - who played  Jazz  in the sitcom from 1990 to 1996 - is a well known DJ.

He was one half of DJ Jazzy Jeff & the Fresh Prince and has since then produced solo music, as well as tunes with Will Smith.

He's worked alongside Mac Miller and Eminem.

Will Smith

Will, 53, shot to fame in 1985 as one half of hip-hop duo DJ Jazzy Jeff & the Fresh Prince.

But he is arguably most known for his portrayal of himself in The Fresh Prince of Bel-Air.

In 1990, TV network NBC created his own sitcom and it became a huge success as fans fell in love with the hilarious scenes, theme tune and how they explored important social issues.

After Will's stint on the show he landed roles in the popular movies Independence Day (1996) and Men in Black (1997).

The actor - who is reportedly worth £255m - is now one of the most successful actors of his time

He's also starred in other blockbusters including I, Robot, The Pursuit of Happyness, I Am Legend, Hancock, Hitch, Suicide Squad and The Karate Kid.

Most recently he produced romantic drama Life in a Year and is due to star in an upcoming thriller called Emancipation.
