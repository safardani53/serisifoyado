Popular sandwich shop offers copycat version of Jersey Mike's dessert and customers can have it for

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Sunday, April 21, 2024

JIMMY John’s has a new menu item as the sandwich chain breaks into the dessert game.

But the treat may look familiar to fans of Jimmy John’s competitor Jersey Mike’s.

The restaurant began serving brownies in May and they’re available nationwide starting at $1.99.

The brownie is made with semi-sweet chocolate, cocoa, and melted chocolate according to Jimmy John’s.

The company called the brownie an “ooey-gooey, delicious dessert option, sure to satisfy the sweet tooth of any sandwich-lover” in a press release.

The new option is 350 calories.

The sandwich chain, popular especially in the Midwest, is known for its cold-cut meats.

It’s not uncommon, however, for sandwich shops to offer small desserts to customers, with other sandwich giants like Subway offering them as well.

The new brownie is served at all locations across 44 states and Washington D.C.

SANDWICHES AND SWEETS

While Jimmy John’s still offers oatmeal raisin and chocolate chip cookies, brownies are new for the chain.

These are not the only baked goods though, as Jimmy John’s offers French Bread — without any sandwich fillings.

At 350 calories, the new brownie is the lightest dessert the chain serves.

Similarly, Jersey Mike’s offers a brownie and a chocolate chip cookie.

Both Jimmy John’s and Jersey Mike’s beat Subway to serve brownies.

However, Subway offers a much broader selection of cookies, including double chocolate, macadamia nut, and raspberry cheesecake.

Subway also offers six and 12-packs of cookies.

Arby’s also lacks a brownie option but has cookies, turnovers, and shakes on offer.

SANDWICH STORIES

Jimmy John’s has been filling up its menu with new items since this spring.

The restaurant added new wraps to its menu this year, with caesar chicken, Thai chicken, and jalapeño ranch chicken wraps offered for $12.50 each.

In Wisconsin, some locations tested out new salads.

Changes abound in the sandwich industry, with Subway adding a new rewards system.
