How much does a sofa bed cost?

As there is so much choice on the market, you don't have to splash the cash to get a decent sofa bed that will last. We've seen great budget options in the region of £200 to £300, our top pick being the Eliza Sofa Bed from Bensons For Beds.
But if your budget can allow for it, there are some incredible bespoke options and more permanent solutions that will cost around £700-£1,500, and our favourite is the Inspire Malton 3 Seater sofa bed from SCS.

Is there an alternative to a sofa bed?

If you want to explore more options other than a sofa bed, then you don't need to settle; there are plenty of solutions out there. One nifty solution is the Jay-Be footstool sofa from Robert Dyas, which takes up much less space and is unassuming, meaning it will fit into any space.

You could also opt for a day bed, which is smaller than your average mattress, but a more permanent option.

Do you need a special mattress for a sofa bed?

Most sofa beds come with thinner and more flexible mattresses than you would find on the average bed, which makes them easier to store away when not in use.

You can opt for sofa beds with pocket-sprung mattresses that are of higher quality. But there are also modular sofa beds that use sofa cushions as a mattress, which is surprisingly comfortable.

Can you sleep on a sofa bed every night?

If you're planning to sleep on a sofa bed every night, we would suggest choosing a full-size sofa bed for maximum comfort.

Look out for a hardwood frame for durability and a comfortable mattress.

The best sofa beds also have storage space where you can keep all the bedding you need too.

What should I look for when buying a sofa bed?

There are a few things you should consider before buying a sofa bed.

Make sure you measure the space accordingly with your chosen bed in mind, considering both its folded and unfolded measurements.

Look out for beds with hardwood frames and pay attention to the fillings: a feather-filled sofa offers good comfort for seating, but if you're planning on regularly using your new sofa as a bed, a foam filling and decent mattress are the way to go.

Where to buy a sofa bed in the UK

The best shop for you will very much depend on your own preference, how quickly you want your sofa bed delivered and your budget.

We suggest you compare prices and styles online before heading to the shops to find your favourite one.

What are the different types of sofa beds?

Sofa beds are generally divided into two main categories - the clic-clac mechanism and the pull-out frame.

The first one gets its name from the sound it makes when the bed is folded and unfolded.

Clic clac sofa beds tend to have fewer cushions and smaller armrests to make it easier to transform them from bed to sofa and vice versa.

They are usually easier to use and cheaper than the pull-out frame version but tend to look more like sofa beds than sofas.

The more traditional pull-out frame usually has a roll‐up mattress that's hidden inside the sofa when not in use.
