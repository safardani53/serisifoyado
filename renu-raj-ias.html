Who is IAS officer Dr. Renu Raj?

Dr. Renu Raj is a well-known Indian Administrative Service (IAS) officer and an M.B.B.S doctor too.

She cracked the 2014 Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) examination and achieved the 2nd rank. Although, she was already a reputed and verified M.B.B.S doctor.

However, she forgot her amazing profession in the dream of an IAS officer. This is what curiosity really means.

Despite being in a stable position, she spends her time cracking India’s toughest exam, UPSC.

Ultimately, she became an IAS officer and defeated lakhs of students. Now, it is time to know how she did this and brightened herself.

Early Life and Education

Dr. Renu Raj was born on 21 May 1987 in Kottayam, Kerala, India. Her father is a retired government servant while her mother is a homemaker.

Dr. Renu started her primary education at S.t Teresa’s Higher Secondary School in Changanassery, Kottayam.

She was a very studious girl from her childhood and was very attractive to studies. Following her great skill, she always tried to top in her life. Dr. Renu later enrolled in a college and did her secondary education.

Dr. Renu Raj Became a Doctor

After completing her basic primary and secondary education, she joined a Government Medical College at Kottayam to pursue her medical graduation.

Her hard work paid her when she finally graduated and earned an M.B.B.S degree. Dr. Renu then started her career as a Doctor.

She was fine with her job and was enjoying her life nicely. But there was something that was always pricking her like a needle. The thing was her dream of IAS officer.

Yes, Dr. Renu Raj always wanted to become an IAS officer but wasn’t able to due to a few undisclosed reasons. But now she was independent financially as well as morally.

Therefore, Dr. Renu Jain decided to enter the very tough field that is UPSC. She was already very skilled and was highly active mentally. This helped her a lot in pursuing her next dream.

IAS Journey and Preparation

Now, Dr. Renu Raj was all set to start her UPSC preparation. She began learning the basics while doing her internship by reading newspapers and journals. Shortly, she started syllabus-based studies.

She continued this formula for the next 6 to 7 months. Thereafter, Dr. Renu started building the basic foundations which were important for the examination. Now, she was preparing full time and very seriously.

During this period, Dr. Renu Raj was studying for about 7 to 8 hours every single day. After Mains, she cut her study time to 3 to 4 hours a day. She was now also studying during her journey home from work.

Dr. Renu Raj finally appeared in the examination in 2014. She chose Malayalam Literature as her optional subject. Dr. Renu was very excited about it as it was her best dream.

Her efforts paid her when the government announced the results. She found herself in the second position and almost cried in happiness.

She became a doctor who cracked India’s toughest exam in her very first attempt and even secured AIR 2.
